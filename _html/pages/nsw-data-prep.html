Practical Example

This page uses the National Survey for Wales (NSW) data preparation workflow to show how estimands, domain sizes, and weights are defined before direct estimation.

The NSW data preparation notebook creates binary indicators from the survey and attaches a Local Authority code, la_code, to each respondent. In this worked example, the target domain is therefore Local Authority.

The choice of domain is partly driven by what spatial information is available. Here, the survey data and the external population tables are available at Local Authority level. In practice, if survey respondents have more granular spatial identifiers, such as LSOA, MSOA, or postcode-derived zones, the choice of domain can be more flexible, provided matching domain sizes and covariates are also available.

1 Indicator Families

The NSW preparation workflow includes indicators from several families:

  • household car access,
  • commuting mode and commuting distance,
  • hybrid and remote working,
  • bus and train use,
  • bus and train satisfaction,
  • walking and cycling frequency.

The examples below focus on three indicators that illustrate common estimand choices.

2 Example 1: Households Without Car Access

Notebook indicator:

no_car = 1 when CarUse indicates no access to a car.

Estimand:

Estimate the share of households without access to a car in each Local Authority.

Component NSW example
Outcome Household has no access to a car
Target population All households
Target domain Local Authority
Domain size Number of households in each Local Authority, household_total
Weight Household grossing weight, scaled from SampleHhWeight
Interpretation “x% of households in this Local Authority do not have access to a car.”

This is a household estimand, so the denominator should be households, not adults. The downstream direct estimate should therefore use a household weight and a household domain size.

Examples:

Estimand Indicator construction
Share of households without access to a car no_car = 1 if CarUse indicates no access to a car
Share of adults who use the bus weekly pt_bus_use = 1 if BusFreq12M = 1
Share of adults who commute by car commute_car = 1 if commute mode is car; non-commuting adults may be coded as 0 if the estimand is adult prevalence
Share of commuters who commute by car commute_car = 1 if commute mode is car, but only commuters are in scope

3 Example 2: Car Commuting

Notebook indicator:

commute_car = 1 when TravWkModeAll4 = 1 or TravWkModeLong = 4.

This survey question is routed through work and commuting questions, so the target population must be chosen carefully.

Possible estimand Target population Domain size Interpretation
Share of adults who commute by car All adults aged 16+ Adult population, pop_16plus “x% of adults in this Local Authority are car commuters.”
Share of commuters who commute by car Commuters Number of commuters “x% of commuters in this Local Authority commute by car.”

The NSW notebook uses the first version for the current workflow: all adults aged 16+ as the target population, pop_16plus as the domain size, and WalesAdultWeight as the weight. That makes the indicator an adult-population prevalence measure, not a proportion among commuters only.

If the aim changes to estimating the share of commuters who use a car, the domain size would need to change to the number of commuters in each Local Authority. If that denominator is not available, the direct estimation method or spatial resolution may need to change.

4 Example 3: Regular Bus Use

Notebook indicator:

pt_bus_use = 1 when BusFreq12M = 1, meaning bus use at least once a week.

Estimand:

Estimate the share of adults who use the bus at least weekly in each Local Authority.

Component NSW example
Outcome Uses the bus at least weekly
Target population All adults aged 16+
Target domain Local Authority
Domain size Adult population in each Local Authority, pop_16plus
Weight WalesAdultWeight
Interpretation “x% of adults in this Local Authority use the bus at least weekly.”

This is different from estimating the share of bus users who use the bus weekly. In the current definition, non-users are part of the adult target population. If the estimand were restricted to public transport users, the denominator and possibly the weight would need to change.

The same process should be repeated for every indicator before calculating direct estimates: define the outcome, target population, domain, domain size, weight, and interpretation.
